History

Line began as a response to disaster. Japan's devastating T≈çhoku earthquake in March 2011 damaged telecommunications infrastructure nationwide, obliging employees at NHN Japan, a unit of Korea's NHN Corp, to rely on Internet-based resources to communicate. The company's engineers developed Line to facilitate this, and the company released their app for public use in June. Line has soared in popularity since then, spreading worldwide.

Initially managed by Naver Japan, the popularity of Line has created a spinoff company dedicated to line services called Line Corporation. They are currently headquartered in Japan and remains a subsidiary of Naver Corporation. Aside from Line Messenger, Line offers mobile games and utility applications to its users. Microtransactions play a main role as it allows users to customize their Line Messenger with various themes as well as liven up their conversations with animated stickers.
